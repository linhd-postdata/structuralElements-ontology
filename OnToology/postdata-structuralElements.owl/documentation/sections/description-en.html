postdata-structuralElements ontology: Description back to ToC

As mentioned in previously sections, the objective of this ontology is to cover the aspects relating to the formal structure of a poem, that is to say, the formal structure of a Redaction of a Poetic Work in the postdata-core ontology .
The ontology prefix is pdstruct

Therefore following classes were modeled:

  • The pdstruct:Line class, that represents a unit of verse that usually ends in a visual or typographic break and generally characterized by its length and meter
    For example: “As armas e os barões assinalados” - from Os Lusíada.
  • The pdstruct:Stanza class, tGroup of lines. Usually, this grouping forms the basic recurring metrical unit of a poem, song or play.
    For example:
    As armas e os barões assinalados,
    Que da ocidental praia Lusitana,
    Por mares nunca de antes navegados,
    Passaram ainda além da Taprobana,
    Em perigos e guerras esforçados,
    Mais do que prometia a força humana,
    E entre gente remota edificaram
    Novo Reino, que tanto sublimaram;.
  • The Token class, a lexical element. This is a base class of Word class and Punctuation class.
  • The Word class, a single distinct meaningful element of writing, used with others (or sometimes alone) to form a sentence and typically shown with a space on either side when written or printed<.br/> For example, the word “life”.
  • The Punctuation class, specific markings, signs and symbols that are used in and around sentences to give them structure and to allow for correct understanding and comprehension.
  • The Syllable class, a single unit of speech sound as written or spoken; specifically, a vowel preceded by zero to three consonants (“awl,” “bring,” “strand”), and followed by zero to four consonants (“too,” “brag,” “gloss,” “stings,” “sixths”).
    It is the reference unit of a literary analysis, in some poetic tradicions.
    For example, the onset and coda of the rhyme vowel.

The structural elements of the Redaction are containers of smaller structures. In the structuralElements ontology, the Redaction contains a mandatory list of lines and also it could contain a list of stanzas if it is necessary. In order to obtain, the lines of each stanza , mandatory constraints have been define to fix the first linea (pdstruct:hasFirstLine exactly 1 pdstruct:Line) and last line (pdstruct:hasLastLine exactly 1 pdstruct:Line) of the stanza, so that they would be extracted taking into account the range of the line index.
Although the line content could be save as a string through the data property content, also the lines (or verses) could have a list of tokens (words and punctuation marks). This feature could give suport to a NLP tokenization tools results.
The elements in these mentioned lists are ordered, and therefore. Those lists have been modeled using The Ordered List Ontology ontology as a specialization of Orderedlist class. The constituent elements are defined as subclasses of Slot and in this way they have the properties that allow to know their position and to establish the following and previous elements.

Use examples

In order to facilitate the understanding of the some classes and properties of this vocabulary, some examples are provided below.

Example 1. Lines and Stanzas

The example presented corresponds to the formal structural information about the PoeticWork "Mo-chion dot bhronnadh, a Bhriain", written by Brian O Corcrain. The information is extracted from Bardic Poetry database

Example description

In order to contextualize, the information about the PoeticWork and one of its manifestations(Redaction) has provided. This redaction can be found at https://bardic.celt.dias.ie/pdf/POEM1400.pdf.
Logically, the center of the example is to manage the structural information. For this, the poem content is showed through the lines content. For this reason, an ordered line list has been created with all of its lines asociated.
In this example, the stanzas of the poem have also been identified. So, an ordered list of stanzas has been created and associated to the Redaction.
In order to access to the lines of a stanza, the properties hasFirstLine and hasLastLine have been used.
The lines, that belongs to the general line list, count with a lineNumber property, and this make posible to obtain the next and previous line. An sparql query could extract the next and previous line using the numberLine information.
However, it would also be possible to obtain the next and previous line through the next and previous properties, but this is not always necessary and the implementation of triples would be more tedious and complicated.


We will show the RDF codes generated with the data of this example. It should be noted that the instances will be represented by their URIs (Uniform Resource Identifier) created according to the following structure <http://postdata.linhd.uned.es/resource/{Class}/{UUID}>.
For example, the Person is identified by <http://postdata.linhd.uned.es/resource/Person/ccc591fe0-7f10-11e9-ac29-f2189886299d>
